Advanced Medical English for Dermatologists

Patient Communication, Empathy, and Medical Terminology

🩺 Medical English Note: In English dermatology, "Areata alopecia" is almost exclusively referred to as Alopecia areata. The exercises below use standard English phrasing to help you sound natural to native-speaking patients and colleagues.

Exercise 2: The Empathetic Doctor (Sentence Rephrasing)

Objective: Rewrite clinical, textbook sentences into warm, clear, and reassuring English that a patient with zero medical background can easily understand.

1. Clinical Statement (Androgenetic alopecia):
"Your diagnosis is androgenetic alopecia. We will initiate topical minoxidil therapy."
Suggested: "You have a very common type of genetic hair thinning. I recommend we start using a topical liquid called minoxidil to help stimulate your hair growth."
2. Clinical Statement (Collagen biostimulator):
"We will administer a collagen biostimulator to induce neocollagenesis and improve cutaneous laxity."
Suggested: "I'd like to suggest an injectable treatment that won't just fill wrinkles today. Instead, it encourages your body to produce its own natural collagen over the next few months, giving your skin a firmer, more youthful look."
3. Clinical Statement (Skin cancer / BCC):
"The biopsy confirms a basal cell carcinoma, which is a skin cancer, so we must perform surgical excision."
Suggested: "The lab results show that the spot we looked at is a very common, highly treatable type of skin cancer. To make sure you're fully healed, we need to do a minor procedure to remove it completely."
4. Clinical Statement (Atopic dermatitis):
"Your child has atopic dermatitis. You must avoid environmental irritants and apply topical calcineurin inhibitors."
Suggested: "Your child has a type of eczema. We need to protect their skin from harsh soaps or rough fabrics, and I'm prescribing a gentle, non-steroid cream to calm the inflammation."
5. Clinical Statement (Psoriasis):
"You have plaque psoriasis. We will prescribe topical corticosteroids and advise liberal emollient therapy."
Suggested: "You have psoriasis, which causes these thick patches. I'm prescribing a medicated cream to reduce the redness, and it's very important to keep the skin heavily moisturized every day."
6. Clinical Statement (Alopecia areata):
"This is alopecia areata. It is an autoimmune etiology and spontaneous regrowth may occur."
Suggested: "You have a condition where the immune system gets confused and makes the hair fall out in patches. The good news is that the hair roots are still alive, and the hair often grows back on its own."
7. Clinical Statement (Botox vs. Fillers):
"We cannot use botulinum toxin for your nasolabial folds; we require hyaluronic acid dermal fillers."
Suggested: "Botox is great for relaxing muscles, but for these deep smile lines, we need to add volume back into the skin. I recommend a gel-like filler to smooth them out."
